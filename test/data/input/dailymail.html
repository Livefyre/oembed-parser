The end of a cinematic era: A look back at the unconventional 13 year relationship of Helena Bonham Carter and Tim Burton... through the movies they made

By Hanna Flint for MailOnline

Follow @HannaInes

Published: 04:13 EST, 24 December 2014 | Updated: 09:35 EST, 24 December 2014

389 shares

42

comments

Their's was a union more unconventional than most, but after 13 years Helena Bonham Carter and Tim Burton have announced their split.

The couple not only shared over a decade together, and two children, but also a string of quirky movies directed by him and starring her.

MailOnline looks back at their life together through the movies they made. 

Scroll down for video 

End of a cinematic era: The quirkiest couple in Hollywood, Helena Bonham Carter and Tim Burton have split up

In 2001, Burton and Bonham Carter met while he directed her in the remake of Planet Of The Apes, co-starring Mark Wahlberg and Alan Cumming.

Upon meeting Helena, Tim's first words to her were: 'I can really see you in an ape mask.'

'He said, "Don't be offended, but you're the first person I thought of for this part. I get the feeling you like to change what you look like." I said, "You're absolutely right,"' the actress explained.

At the time the director was still in a relationship with actress and model Lisa Marie Smith who acted in the films he made during their relationship from 1992 to 2001, most notably in Ed Wood and Mars Attacks!.

'I can really see you in an ape mask': Tim and Helena first met on the set of Planet Of The Apes in 2001

The beginning: Tim and Helena at the New York premiere of the film. Tim was in a relationship with Lisa Marie Smith at the time but ended it not long before starting a romance with the British actress

Lisa Marie attended a number of premieres for the movie with Tim, but they soon split and he and Helena embarked on a romance. Smith showed just how amicable the break up was by auctioning off the belongings he left behind a few years later, to his dismay.

Both Burton and Bonham Carter denied any sort of overlap between relationships.

2003's Big Fish was the first film they worked on together as a couple, a story about a son trying to find out more about his dying father. 

First: 2003's Big Fish marked their first collaboration as a couple. Here they are pictured with Ewan McGregor at the UK gala premiere in January 2004

Pregnant witch: Helena was carrying her son Billy Ray during the production

The script personally appealed to Burton who was never close to his parents; however the loss of his father in October 2000 and his mother two years later affected him deeply.

Helena was pregnant with their first son Billy Ray at the time, and played two roles, one of which was a witch. 'I was pregnant throughout filming, so it was weird being a pregnant witch,' the actress said. 'I had morning sickness, so all those fumes and the make-up and the rubber...it was hideous.'

She gave birth to her first son in 2003, and by that time Tim had moved from his hometown of Los Angeles to London having already an apartment in Hampstead Heath from when he directed Sleepy Hollow.

Mrs. Bucket: Helena took on the role of Charlie's mother in Tim's remake of Charlie & The Factory

Going strong: The couple at the UK premiere of the film.  The American director had moved to London by this point

But he soon bought the Belsize Park house next door to the one Helena owned, beginning their unusual living arrangement. 

Two years later, the couple were back working together on two films, first the Charlie And The Chocolate Factory remake with Johnny Depp as Willy Wonka and Helena as Mrs Bucket; followed up by the animation, Corpse Bride.

Bonham Carter played the title character in the film, the third stop-motion feature produced by Burton and the first directed by him (The Nightmare Before Christmas and James And The Giant Peach were directed by Henry Selick).

Animated: Helena took on the title role in Tim's first stop-motion animation as a director, Corpse Bride

Critically-acclaimed: Burton was nominated for Best Animated Feature Film at the 78th annual Academy Awards in 2006 and was accompanied by Helena

The couple began trying for another baby, but as she was in her very late 30s Helena struggled to conceive.

The actress finally feel pregnant naturally two years later in 2007, aged 41, while working on the Stephen Sondheim musical Sweeney Todd: The Demon Barber Of Fleet Street, directed by her partner, though she told the Daily Telegraph that they had considered IVF.

'The first three months being pregnant while filming, I felt totally spaced out,' she told the newspaper.

'You do need to multi-task with acting. You've got to remember your marks, your lines, singing, everything, and actually – you have no brain! Suddenly your own brain is growing another person's brain, so yours goes defunct. It wasn't ideal, but then I was so happy to be pregnant.'

The Worst Pies In London: Helena was pregnant with her second child while filming her role as Mrs. Lovett in the 2007 Sweeney Todd: The Demon Barber Of Fleet Street 

Struggle: It took the couple two years to conceive their second child, and Helena said they considered IVF treatment 

In December the same year she gave birth to a daughter Nell, named for the 'Helens' in her family.

The couple were still living in separated houses, though it seemed to be more down to getting a good night sleep than anything else.

'There's a snoring issue... I talk, he snores,' she said. 'The other thing is, he's an insomniac, so he needs to watch television to get to sleep while I need silence.

'Having two houses means we can get out of each other's hair - which, let's face it, we've both got a lot of!'

Two becomes one: In 2011, the couple filed for planning permission to knock through a wall joining their houses in Belsize Park

Three years later, Tim and Helena returned to work together on the live-action Alice In Wonderland in which the actress played the Red Queen - a part she said she based on her little girl.

Of working with her partner, Helena said upon the film's release: ‘It’s not without its pressures, working with Tim. It worked on Alice. Sweeney was very stressful, very hard on our relationship. He’s a partner in our private life, but when he’s directing, he’s the boss. And maybe I confuse that.’

In 2011, the couple filed for planning permission to knock through a wall between their houses to make an easy walk way between their separate abodes.

‘He always visits, which is really touching,' Helena said. 'He’s always coming over. We have a throughway. Journalists think there’s an underground tunnel, gothic. It’s actually quite above ground, lots of light.’ 

Red Queen: Helena starred in Tim's 2010's live-action remake of Alice In Wonderland

Tough: Helena said during the press tour for the Disney film: ‘It’s not without its pressures, working with Tim'

In 2012, Tim brought the old TV series Dark Shadows to the big screen, with Bonham Carter in the role of psychiatrist Dr. Julia Hoffman, marking their seventh and last movie as a couple.

A year later, the first signs of trouble in their relationship were observed, when Burton, 56, was pictured embracing a mystery blonde, thought to be one of his assistants, near his home in Hampstead. At the time the couple declined to comment.

Now a spokesperson has confirmed Helena and Tim have brought their quirky partnership to an end: ‘Tim Burton and Helena Bonham Carter separated amicably earlier this year and have continued to be friends and co-parent their children.'

A friend of the couple told the Daily Mail: 'Helena was very upset at the Chequers party earlier this month hosted by Samantha Cameron.

Final: In 2012, Tim brought the old TV series Dark Shadows to the big screen, with Bonham Carter in the role of psychiatrist Dr. Julia Hoffman, marking their seventh and last movie as a couple

Trouble? A year after the release of Dark Shadows, Burton, 56, was pictured embracing a mystery blonde, thought to be one of his assistants, near his home in Hampstead

‘Other guests noticed she was in a bad way. She is really upset. It’s all still very raw and they only told the children two weeks ago.’

Another friend said: ‘Tim is having a classic mid-life crisis. All Helena’s friends think he’s a terrible old cliche. Helena gave him a lot of time to sort himself out.’

But it's not quite the end of the two as Helena has already reprised her role for the Alice In Wonderland sequel, which Tim is producing.

So while the flames of romance has extinguished for the two, their cinematic partnership will hopefully continue to burn just as bright.

Amicable: A spokesperson confirmed the split on Tuesday, saying: ‘Tim Burton and Helena Bonham Carter separated amicably earlier this year and have continued to be friends and co-parent their children'
