Welcome to our live coverage of day three of competition at the 2016 Rio Olympics.

  • 1:29pm on 9 Aug 2016

Day three recap

RUGBY: Australia's women's sevens team have beaten New Zealand 24-17 in the final to clinch gold (Story from Andrew Webster).

SWIMMING:  

Emily Seebohm finish seventh in the final of the women's 100m backstroke, with Madi Wilson coming eighth. Mitch Larkin finishes fourth in 100m backstroke (Story from Phil Lutton). 

America's Lilly King wins 100m breastroke while Sun Yang claims 200m freestyle crown (Story from Phil Lutton). 

Emma McKeon (6th) and Bronte Barratt (8th) through to 200m freestyle final. 

Alicia Coutts qualifies sixth for 400m IM final. Grant Irvine misses out on 200m butterfly final. 

BEACH VOLLEYBALL: Lou Bawden & Taliqua Clancy down Venezuela 21-9, 21-14. 

BASKETBALL: The Boomers accounted for Serbia 95-80 thanks to a star performance from Matthew Dellavedova who made 23 points and 11 assists (Story from Michael Chammas). 

HOCKEY: Australia lose to USA as coach Adam Commens hits out (Story from Malcolm Knox). 

WATER POLO: Australia drew 9-9 with Hungary (Story from Greg Baum). 

CYCLING: Mel Hoskins suffers horror training crash (Story from Sam Lane). 

SWIMMING: Grant Irvine qualified fourth fastest (1:55.64) for the semi-finals of the men's 200 metres butterfly, 0.09 in front of Olympic legend Michael Phelps (Story from Chris Barrett). He is not in finals contention now. 

TENNIS: Sam Stosur eliminates Japan's Misaki Doi 6-3 6-4 to progress to the third round. John Millman goes down 7-6, 6-4 to No.4 seed Kei Nishikori after getting an early break in the first set (Story from Michael Chammas). 

KAYAKING: Jessica Fox negotiates difficult Rio course to make semis (Story from Mick Gleeson). 

Other news...

Stray bullet that hit tent housing media contingent came from a nearby favela, authorities say

Bolt says he will break 19s for 200m

Brazilian Rafaela Silva wins first gold medal for hosts in judo and bursts into tears

Australia on verge of equestrian gold

That's all from me, see you again bright and early tomorrow. Cheers

Seebohm sinks to seventh

Australia's backstroke world champion Emily Seebohm has finished seventh in the final of the women's 100m backstroke as Hungary's Iron Lady Katinka Hosszu claimed another gold medal in the Rio pool.

Seebohm took silver in London and went hard early to take the lead from lane one, only to be hauled in by all except teammate Madi Wilson, who rounded out the field.

Sun Yang wins 200m free

Sun Yang became the first Chinese swimmer to win the Olympic men's 200 metres freestyle gold medal on Monday.

Sun, silver medallist in the event in London four years ago, touched the wall in one minute, 44.65 seconds to beat South Africa's Chad Le Clos by 0.55 seconds. Conor Dwyer of the United States won bronze.

  • 11:50am on 9 Aug 2016

Bolt says he will break 19 seconds for 200m

The world's fastest man believes, or hopes, but with Usain Bolt you take it as an article of faith, that his last Olympic goal will be achieved in Rio and he will become the first man to break 19 seconds for the 200 metres.

Dismissive of the idea he might be below his best after a hamstring injury, Bolt said that, on the contrary, he was chasing his last remaining goal - the sub-19 200m.
